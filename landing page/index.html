     Coca-Cola, or Coke, is a carbonated soft drink manufactured by the Coca-Cola Company. In 2013, Coke products were sold in over 200 countries worldwide, with consumers drinking more than 1.8 billion company beverage servings each day. Coca-Cola ranked No.87 in the 2018 Fortune 500 list of the largest United States corporations by total revenue. Based on Interbrand's "best global brand" study of 2020, Coca-Cola was the world's sixth most valuable brand.

History

The Origin Of Coca Cola

   On May 8, 1886, Dr. John Pemberton brought his perfected syrup to Jacobs' Pharmacy in downtown Atlanta where the first glass of Coca‑Cola was poured. Serving about nine drinks per day in its first year, Coca‑Cola was an exciting new drink in the beginning. See the story here of how it all began.

Coca‑Cola’s Sustainability History

   Sustainability is not something new at The Coca‑Cola Company. Since 1917, our efforts have covered a wide range of topics including: water sustainability, women empowerment, community well-being, sustainable packaging, climate protection, human and workplace rights, and sustainable agriculture.

Diet Coke

First introduced in the United States in 1982 as a response to the growing demand for sugar-free drinks. Since then, Diet Coke has become one of the most successful diet soft drinks in the world, with sales in more than 150 countries.

Zero Coke

 Sugar-free soft drink produced by The Coca-Cola Company, and it was first introduced in 2005. It is marketed as a healthier alternative to regular Coca-Cola, with no sugar or calories. Zero Coke is made with artificial sweeteners, primarily aspartame and acesulfame potassium, which provide a sweet taste without the calories of regular sugar.

Cinnamon

 Coca-Cola Cinnamon is a limited-edition soft drink produced by The Coca-Cola Company. It was first introduced in the United States in 2019 as a holiday season flavor, and it has since been released in other countries as well. Coca-Cola Cinnamon is a variation of the original Coca-Cola flavor, with added cinnamon flavoring.

Cherry

  Coca-Cola Cherry is a popular variation of the classic Coca-Cola flavor, with added cherry flavoring. It has become a well-known and widely consumed soft drink, and it offers consumers a refreshing and fruity alternative to regular cola.

Vanilla

  Coca-Cola Vanilla is a popular variation of the classic Coca-Cola flavor, with added vanilla flavoring. It has become a well-known and widely consumed soft drink, and it offers consumers a smooth and creamy alternative to regular cola.

Rasbery

 Rasberry soft drink produced by The Coca-Cola Company, and it was first introduced in 2005. It is marketed as a healthier alternative to regular Coca-Cola, with no sugar or calories. Rasberry Coke is made with artificial sweeteners, primarily aspartame and acesulfame potassium, which provide a sweet taste without the calories of regular sugar.

Peach

 Coca-Cola Peach is a variation of the classic Coca-Cola flavor, with added peach flavoring. It is made with natural flavors and includes the same ingredients as regular Coca-Cola, including high-fructose corn syrup (or sugar in some markets), caramel color, and phosphoric acid.
